Design patterns originated in architecture. Christopher Alexander et al abstracts a template solution to common solutions that he encountered in his field. This inspired four programmers to write a classical design patterns book for OOP. This soon became a standard for software engineering, where people implemented design patterns into their project and using it to abstract solutions out of common problems and this topic is broadly implemented by all projects, and it became a standard, and everyone lived happily ever after…

But that’s too complicated (and boring), even for me who is studying web application design. Perhaps a better explainaition could be found in the analogy of generational traditions. To me, there are many parrallels to a folk tale a cautionary story. Common sayings, or in this case, a common solutions among the community became solidified in the public concious. These solutions, tried and tested by many unfortunate software developers beforehand, became formalized guideline, similar to how a commonly practiced tradition slowly integrated into a society as an unspoken law.

Perhaps describing the parrallels between patterns and traditions seems totally unrelated and takes away from learning how using them, but understanding the relation between the two could allow for effective application of these design patterns. Both subjects shares the same purpose, to solves problems that have been solve many times before. Traditions, like [], gives people a guideline on this common problem. Design patterns also prevent people from spending time to solve the same problems that have been solved before, or even how to solve them efficiently. Both are essentially a mechanism our community used to stop people from reinventing the wheel again.

And with software engineering, reinventing the wheel is a common pitfall. Common problems or a variation of them pop up times and times again. Something like authentication, [what ever is MVC is trying to solve], improving functionality, etc are something that has been solved before, currently, and will keep being solved again and again til the end of time. 

With this, frameworks are essential a design pattern. Frameworks, like react and meteor, are tools that are advertised as a foundation or a tool set that allows ones to build a web app. So, the project for my 314 class has many design patterns; authentication, MVC, and singleton; built-in.

This idea is eye opening for a software engineer. (When looked this way, design patterns also becomes…) / Tools and frameworks are essentially design patterns for other software engineers.

Design pattern, in a more practical sense, becomes a method of creating a tool or method that saves time for other programmers. A good example is building [factory class/interface] for your component. In my ICS 314 class, my assigned functionality often need a way to interface with other components. Rather than hard coding my component into other people’s code or made them learn how to pass in props for my components, I made a simple interface component. It contains the bare arguments nescessary for the component, and it would pull the rest of the required properties within the function it self. Rather than solving the problem of integrating my component in multiple places, I created a template solution that me and my teammate could use instead.
