If someone were to ask what made me join the computer science program at the University of Hawaii, I wouldn't want to answer them. Like many of my peers, I came into the major because my interests in video games. I don't have a specific video game title that could be the sole cause for pushing my life in its current direction. instead, this interest spurs from the collection of titles released during the early 2000s. Seeing many game companies becoming successful and making a lot of money at the time also helped with choosing to enroll in U.H. as a Computer Science major. My game plan at the time was to get a computer science degree, then start working as a game designer at one of the popular companies, like Valve or Mojang.

Like all plans, my desired career path changed when I came into contact with the ICS program at U.H. at Manoa. At first, I was disinterested with the required introductory classes of the program. "Using Java and learning discrete math isn't necessary to becoming a game developer!," I thought. After pouting for sometime, I slowly began to saw how my classes, despite being unrelated, could help me to reach my desired career. Each computer science topics, like memory management and algorithms, provide a foundation of general programming that I could use towards game programming.

The program also helped me see the reality of being a game developer, working through stressful crunches and being exploited by game publishers. There are many people like who also want to work at a game studio, and there is a large amount of potential game designer who didn't see the reality of software development. Some are willing to work in a terrible environment, being used to make profits for the publishers, just because they are making video games. While I'm not completely turned-off from my original goal, I have a set of possible jobs that I could still do.

The more varied things I learn, the better I become at game designing and programming in general.

With each classes I take and each thing I learn within the program, I know that: even if this won't help me in my initial goal, I would be able to use it on whatever I want in the future. With this assurance, I'm seeking to learn as much new topics, frameworks, or tools as I can. U.H at Manoa provides a good catalog of classes and student clubs as well.
