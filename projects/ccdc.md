[CCDC](https://www.nationalccdc.org/) is my first exposure to a live cybersecurity competition. Given a virtual computer network, my team must try to secure it against outside attacks, implement a safe administrative policies, and fix any vulnerability to the system. The competition is set in a live roleplay environment, and we played a role of a fictional company's I.T. team for a period of two days. The given network of computers is composed of 8 to 10 virtual machines that are setup to work like actual infrastructure used in a company. In addition to maintaining the system, there would be a group of competition volunteers, called the yellow team, who would requests system changes that would force us to revises our strategies. Another group of volunteers, the red team, would also constantly attack our system with real-world exploits and hacking tools for the entire duration of the competition. Overall, this is a high-pressure event that requires adaptability and teamwork.

My role was an assistance Linux administrator in the back-up team, so I've only participated in the qualifying round of the competition. However, I still learned a lot about maintaining Linux computers during training and the qualifying round. My teammate have varied set of skills and I was able to gain an introductory skills from working with them. A particular moment that I remembered is my time working on a difficult problem with a graduate teammate. He was able to do a reality-check on the issue by explaining his thinking to an nontechnical person like me, and I suggested some general, but outside-the-box solution to the problem. This and other similar moments allowed me to learn about a vast, difficult subject of system administration, while also being a complete beginner in the field. In 2020, I want to train harder for the competition to be placed in the main team.
