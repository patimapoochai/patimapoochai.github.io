The [Hawaii Annual Code Challenge](https://hacc.hawaii.gov/) is a hackathon event hosted in Honolulu. The aim of the event was to inspire innovation within the state and allowing local developer, engineers, and students to create new solutions to problems within Hawaii. Participants, ranging from high school students to professional developers, are given problems by the Hawaii government or local businesses. They must develop a solution in a span of a month and present it to a panel of judges, including the governor of Hawaii, David Ige.

<div class="ui centered rounded images">
  <div style="margin: 3%;"><img class="ui image" src="../images/hacc2019_screenshot1.jpg"></div>
</div>

Governor David Ige aimed to push for sustainability within Hawaii, and the challenge that my team have chosen include a theme of spreading awareness for sustainability. We chose to work on the Department of Education's curriculum database, using [Django](https://www.djangoproject.com/), [ArcGIS](https://www.esri.com/en-us/arcgis/about-arcgis/overview) and [Firebase](https://firebase.google.com/). My role in the team was front-end and U.I. designer, making mockups for the front-end of the system. I've also made the logo for the application, which was used as the branding for the project.

<div class="ui centered rounded images">
  <div style="margin: 3%;"><img class="ui image" src="../images/hacc2019_screenshot3.jpg"></div>

This project was my first introduction to the website development cycle. I've learned about the division of front-end, back-end, and database roles, the process to make a web application, and the different tools required. In the next HACC, I want to learn more about back-end development and furthering my familiarity with U.I. design.
